Oboe

About This Instrument

The oboe is a woodwind instrument. When referring to an “oboe”, most people are referring to the soprano oboe, but there are many other instruments in this group, like the English horn or the bass oboe. These instruments use a double reed, or two pieces of wood tied together, and an adequate amount of air to produce a bright sound with their instrument. The instrument appeared in Europe in the mid-17th century and was called a hautbois prior to its current name. Professionals usually make their own reeds due to the specific needs of each piece. Another instrument in the oboe family, the heckelphone, has only been made 165 times, making them extremely rare.
