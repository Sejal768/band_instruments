Tuba

About This Instrument

The tuba is the lowest-pitched instrument in a band, which makes it one of the most important. It gained popularity around the mid-19th century which makes it so much newer than other instruments, but because of its low pitch and mellow sound, it is the backbone for many great band or orchestral pieces. Players buzz their lips into the mouthpiece like any other brass player, but this instrument produces a lower sound due to its size and length. An average tuba, uncoiled, would reach up to 18 feet! The more air has to travel, the lower the pitch would be, so 18 feet is a lot. Because of its weight and size, the tuba is extremely hard to carry, let alone march with, so a sousaphone is the instrument in place for the tuba.
