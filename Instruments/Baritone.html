Baritone

About This Instrument

The baritone, or euphonium, is a three or four-valved instrument used in bands but very rarely orchestras. It is a middle-sized instrument with piston valves but rotary valves in euphoniums do exist. The baritone and euphonium are very similar except for the bell size, which is smaller in the baritone than the euphonium. Because the instrument is relatively new compared to others, it is not generally used in orchestras but very common in marching bands. The euphonium/baritone is basically a smaller version of the tuba, although much more portable, light, and easier to carry. It gained popularity around the 19th century and is still a commonly used instrument today.
