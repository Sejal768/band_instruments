Flute

About This Instrument

The flute is a woodwind instrument. While other woodwind instruments use a single or double reed to make a sound, the flute uses no reed. Instead, it makes a sound with a vibrating column of air. The instrument originated somewhere in central Asia, but was introduced to Germany around the 17th century. While many flutes were made of wood and had tone holes, a German blacksmith and musician named Theobald Boehm modified the flute and included keys, pads, mechanics, etc. This flute became known as the concert flute and joined a whole family of flutes which includes: piccolos, altos, bamboos, etc. These flutes are still widely played all around the world.
