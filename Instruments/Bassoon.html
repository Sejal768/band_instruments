Bassoon

About This Instrument

The bassoon is a woodwind instrument. It is also a double-reeded instrument which most commonly plays in the tenor or bass clef. The bassoon is concert pitched and has two forms: the French and the German. It is the largest woodwind instrument and typically needs many harnesses and straps to play due to its size and complex fingerings. Its range is about 3 octaves, but pieces written for this instrument rarely include notes above C5 or D5. The instrument is one of the most complex in terms of the constructing of pieces, and it has 6 parts including the reed. Professional bassoon players may make their own reed due to the specific requirements of a music score.
